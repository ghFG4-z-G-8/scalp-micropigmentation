Frequently Asked Questions

What is scalp micropigmentation and how does it create a buzz cut look?
Scalp micropigmentation (SMP) is a non-invasive cosmetic procedure that uses specialized microneedles to deposit pigment into the scalps upper layers, creating the illusion of a closely shaved head or buzz cut.[2] The technique strategically applies pigment dots that replicate the appearance of natural hair follicles, resulting in a realistic shaved head aesthetic.[2] Unlike surgical hair restoration methods, SMP requires no surgery or lengthy recovery periods.[3]
How many sessions does scalp micropigmentation typically require?
Most people require 2-3 treatment sessions to achieve optimal results, with each session lasting up to five hours.[3] Following each session, specific aftercare instructions must be followed during the healing period.[3]
What hair length should I maintain to complement my SMP treatment?
A buzz cut is an ideal length to pair with scalp micropigmentation, typically ranging from a #1 guard to no guard on clipper settings.[5] Alternatively, a razor shave cut provides a sleek, clean appearance that works especially well for covering baldness or patchiness.[5] The SMP application is designed conservatively so that if you decide to cut your hair shorter, the pigmentation will still blend seamlessly.[5]
What are the main benefits of choosing SMP over surgical hair restoration?
SMP offers several advantages over surgical options: it provides immediate results without waiting months for improvement, requires no surgery or long recovery periods, is more cost-effective than hair transplants, and demands minimal maintenance compared to other hair loss solutions.[3] Additionally, it delivers natural-looking, realistic results that can significantly enhance confidence and self-esteem.[3]
How is density scalp micropigmentation different from buzz cut style SMP?
Density SMP aims to enhance the appearance of hair density and fullness by strategically applying pigments to create the illusion of thicker, fuller hair.[1] Buzz cut style SMP, by contrast, focuses on creating the appearance of a closely shaved or buzzed hairstyle, with uniform pigment application that replicates a freshly trimmed buzz cut, regardless of the individuals actual hair density.[1]
What should I do to prepare my scalp before an SMP procedure?
Before your SMP procedure, shave your head against the grain for a close shave to remove all hair stubble.[2] Keep your scalp clean and dry, and avoid getting it wet during the preparation period.[3] A smooth, even canvas allows the technician to place pigment dots with precision, ensuring natural and even distribution that seamlessly mimics a shaved head look.[2]
