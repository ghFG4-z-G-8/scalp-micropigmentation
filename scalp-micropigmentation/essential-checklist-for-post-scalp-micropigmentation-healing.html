Frequently Asked Questions

How long does scalp micropigmentation take to heal?
The healing process typically takes **2-4 weeks**, with the majority of healing occurring within the first month[1][4]. Complete healing of the scalp can take up to **4-6 weeks**[3]. Most people experience no downtime and can return to work within a day or two after treatment[2].
What should I expect during the first week after scalp micropigmentation?
During **days 1-5**, expect redness, minor swelling, and tiny scabs forming over each treated area[1]. By **days 6-10**, the initial redness fades and scabs begin to flake away naturally, though the pigment may appear lighter—this is completely normal[1]. Avoid touching, washing, or sweating the area, and resist picking or scratching the scabs[1].
When will the pigment color settle to its final shade?
The pigment will appear **darker than the desired outcome** immediately after treatment due to extra pigment on the scalp and increased blood flow[5]. It will **soften within 1-2 weeks** as the skin heals[5]. Over the first **4-6 weeks**, the initial dark appearance gradually settles to its final shade as swelling subsides and the skin fully heals[3].
What aftercare steps are most important for optimal healing?
Keep the treated area **clean and dry**, avoid direct sunlight, and do not apply products during the first few days[6]. Use a cold compress to reduce swelling, wear a hat if needed, and moisturize with products like grape seed oil to prevent dryness and itching[4][5]. Most importantly, follow all aftercare instructions from your SMP specialist and avoid scratching, picking, or scrubbing the treated area[2][4].
Is it normal to experience itching and dryness during healing?
Yes, **dryness, flaking, and itchiness are completely normal** during the healing process[5]. Most clients experience itchiness around **days 5-7** as the body heals the tiny wounds from the micro-needles[6]. This typically subsides as the skin continues to regenerate, and moisturizing can help alleviate these symptoms[6].
