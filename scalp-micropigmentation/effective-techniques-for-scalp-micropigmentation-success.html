Frequently Asked Questions

What is scalp micropigmentation?
Scalp micropigmentation is a non-surgical procedure that deposits tiny pigment dots into the scalps upper dermal layer to mimic hair follicles and create the illusion of density for those experiencing hair loss or baldness.[2]
How can artists improve their scalp micropigmentation technique for better results?
Artists can enhance technique by understanding scalp anatomy, practicing with different needle configurations, developing consistent hand pressure, creating uniform dot sizes, and ensuring symmetry and balance in hairline design.[1]
What preparation steps ensure scalp micropigmentation success?
Prepare by moisturizing the scalp for at least 21 days beforehand, getting a haircut if needed, matching scalp tone by avoiding uneven tanning, and being honest in consultation about medical history and goals.[3][4][7]
What aftercare is essential after scalp micropigmentation for long-lasting results?
For the first 4 days, avoid wetting, sweating, sun exposure, and touching the scalp; from day 4, moisturize daily, use sunscreen, avoid alcohol-heavy products, and follow specialist instructions to promote healing and pigment retention.[3][5][6]
How to choose a specialist for successful scalp micropigmentation?
Select a skilled specialist experienced in medical tattooing with a portfolio of natural-looking results, research credentials, ask pre-treatment questions, and ensure they customize design and color matching to your needs.[2][8]
