Ignoring Keloid-Prone Skin

Individuals prone to keloids, where scars grow larger than normal, should avoid scalp micropigmentation as it can trigger their formation on the scalp.[1][2][3] Consult a dermatologist beforehand to assess your skin type and history of scarring. Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common side effects of scalp micropigmentation?
The most common side effects include redness, itchiness, tenderness, and swelling, which typically appear immediately after treatment and subside within 1-2 days[3][4]. Some clients may also experience itchy scalp a couple of days after the procedure as part of the natural healing process[4]. These mild side effects are completely normal and temporary[3].
Can scalp micropigmentation cause scarring, and how can I prevent it?
While scalp micropigmentation is non-invasive, there is a slight risk of scarring if needles penetrate too deeply or if the healing process is not properly managed[2]. To minimize scarring risk, follow post-treatment care instructions diligently and work with an experienced, licensed practitioner[2][5]. People prone to keloids should avoid the procedure entirely, as it can trigger keloid formation[5][6].
What are the risks of choosing an inexperienced or unlicensed practitioner?
Using an unlicensed or inexperienced provider significantly increases risks, including allergic reactions to pigments, unnatural appearance, infection from improperly sterilized needles, blowouts (pigment deposited too deeply), and poor color results[5][6]. An inexperienced technician may also create blotchy results or deposit pigment at incorrect depths[1][3].
How should I care for my scalp after scalp micropigmentation to avoid complications?
After treatment, avoid direct sunlight, hot showers, shampooing, and bathing for several days[1]. Keep the scalp away from contact and sun exposure, as this may increase blood flow and affect healing[1]. Follow your practitioners specific aftercare instructions carefully to prevent infection and other complications[2][3].
What should I do if I experience signs of infection after scalp micropigmentation?
Contact your doctor immediately if you notice signs of infection, including redness, swelling, heat, or pus at the treatment site[3]. Infection is preventable through good hygiene and proper aftercare, but if symptoms develop, professional medical attention is necessary[3].
How long does scalp micropigmentation last, and will the pigment fade?
Scalp micropigmentation typically retains its appearance for four years or longer[6]. However, the pigment may fade after three to five years due to sun exposure and other external factors[1]. Regular touch-ups may be required to maintain the desired appearance over time[2].
